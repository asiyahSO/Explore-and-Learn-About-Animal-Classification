NILE CROCODILE

The Nile Crocodile (Crocodylus niloticus) is one of the largest and most powerful crocodilian species native to the Nile River basin and sub-Saharan Africa. Known for their armored skin and formidable jaws, Nile crocodiles are apex predators feeding on fish, birds, mammals, and sometimes even large prey like buffalo. They inhabit rivers, lakes, and marshlands, using stealth and sudden bursts of speed to catch prey. Despite their fearsome reputation, Nile crocodiles play a vital role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: NILE CROCODILE
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: CROCODYLUS NILOTICUS
  • TYPE: REPTILES
  • SIZE: 13 TO 16 FEET (Length)
  • WEIGHT: 500 TO 1,650 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 70 TO 100 YEARS
