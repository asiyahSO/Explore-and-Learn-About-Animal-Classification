COMET GOLDFISH

The Comet Goldfish (Carassius auratus) is a popular ornamental freshwater fish originally from China, now bred worldwide in captivity. Known for its long, flowing fins and slender body, the comet goldfish comes in various colors including red, orange, white, and calico. They are hardy fish that can adapt to a range of water conditions, making them favorites for beginner aquarists. Comets feed on flakes, pellets, and live foods and can grow up to 12 inches in length.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: COMET GOLDFISH
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: CARASSIUS AURATUS
  • TYPE: FISH
  • SIZE: UP TO 12 INCHES
  • WEIGHT: UP TO 1 POUND
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 10 TO 15 YEARS
