GALAPAGOS TORTOISE

The Galapagos Tortoise (Chelonoidis nigra) is the largest species of tortoise found exclusively on the Galápagos Islands in Ecuador. These giant tortoises are famous for their massive size, slow movements, and long lifespan, often exceeding 100 years. They feed mainly on grasses, cacti, and fruits. Galápagos tortoises played a key role in Charles Darwin’s theory of evolution due to the variations observed among different island populations. Conservation efforts have been essential to protect them from threats like invasive species and habitat loss.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: GALAPAGOS TORTOISE
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: CHELONOIDIS NIGRA
  • TYPE: REPTILES
  • SIZE: UP TO 5 FEET (Length)
  • WEIGHT: UP TO 550 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 100+ YEARS
