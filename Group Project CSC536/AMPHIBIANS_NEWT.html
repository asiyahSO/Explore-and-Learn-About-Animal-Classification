EASTERN NEWT

The Eastern Newt (Notophthalmus viridescens) is a common amphibian found in the eastern United States and Canada. It has a complex life cycle with three distinct stages: aquatic larvae, a bright orange terrestrial juvenile phase (known as the red eft), and an aquatic adult with greenish-brown skin and red spots. Eastern newts inhabit ponds, lakes, and moist forests and feed on insects, worms, and small aquatic organisms. They produce mild skin toxins as a defense mechanism.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: EASTERN NEWT
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: NOTOPHTHALMUS VIRIDESCENS
  • TYPE: AMPHIBIANS
  • SIZE: LESS THAN 1 OUNCE
  • WEIGHT: 4 TO 8 OUNCES
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 12 TO 15 YEARS
