INDIAN COBRA

The Indian Cobra (Naja naja) is a venomous snake species native to the Indian subcontinent. Famous for its iconic hood, which it flares when threatened, the Indian cobra is a symbol in Indian culture and mythology. It primarily feeds on rodents, frogs, and other small animals, using potent neurotoxic venom to immobilize prey. Cobras are typically found in forests, agricultural fields, and urban areas, often close to human settlements. Despite their dangerous bite, they generally avoid confrontation with humans.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: INDIAN COBRA
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: NAJA NAJA
  • TYPE: REPTILES
  • SIZE: 3 TO 6 FEET (Length)
  • WEIGHT: 3 TO 6 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 20 YEARS
