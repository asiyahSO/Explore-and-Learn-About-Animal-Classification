MEXICAN AXOLOTL

The Mexican Axolotl (Ambystoma mexicanum) is a unique amphibian native to lakes around Mexico City. Unlike most amphibians, axolotls remain in their larval form throughout life, retaining gills and a fully aquatic lifestyle, a phenomenon called neoteny. They are famous for their regenerative abilities, capable of regrowing limbs, spinal cord, and even parts of the heart and brain. Axolotls are critically endangered in the wild due to pollution and habitat destruction, though they are common in labs and aquariums.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: MEXICAN AXOLOTL
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: AMBYSTOMA MEXICANUM
  • TYPE: AMPHIBIANS
  • SIZE: 6 TO 18 INCHES
  • WEIGHT: 2 TO 8 OUNCES
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 10 TO 15 YEARS
