FRESHWATER ANGELFISH

The Freshwater Angelfish (Pterophyllum scalare) is a striking freshwater fish native to the Amazon River Basin in South America. Known for its triangular body shape and long, elegant fins, angelfish display various color patterns including silver, black, and marble. They inhabit slow-moving waters with dense vegetation and feed on small invertebrates and plant matter. Freshwater angelfish are popular in home aquariums for their graceful appearance and peaceful temperament.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: FRESHWATER ANGELFISH
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: PTEROPHYLLUM SCALARE
  • TYPE: FISH
  • SIZE: UP TO 6 INCHES (Body length)
  • WEIGHT: UP TO 4 OUNCES
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 10 TO 12 YEARS
