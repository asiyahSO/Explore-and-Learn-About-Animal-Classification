BLUE POISON DART FROG

The Blue Poison Dart Frog (Dendrobates tinctorius “azureus”) is a brightly colored amphibian native to the tropical rainforests of Colombia in South America. Known for its vibrant cobalt-blue skin with black spots, this tiny frog warns predators of its toxicity through its color. It produces potent toxins through its skin, although captive-bred frogs are typically non-toxic due to a different diet. Blue poison dart frogs are active during the day and feed on small insects. They are a popular species in zoos and conservation programs.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: BLUE POISON DART FROG
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: Dendrobates tinctorius “azureus”
  • TYPE: AMPHIBIANS
  • SIZE: 1 TO 2 INCHES
  • WEIGHT: LESS THAN 1 OUNCE
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 4 TO 6 YEARS (Wild), up to 10 years in captivity
