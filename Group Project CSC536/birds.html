BIRDS

Birds are warm-blooded animals characterized by feathers, beaks without teeth, and the ability to lay hard-shelled eggs. They are known for their strong, lightweight skeletons and most have the power of flight, though some species like penguins and ostriches are flightless. Birds inhabit nearly every environment on Earth, from icy polar regions to tropical rainforests and deserts. Birds display a wide range of sizes and behaviors from tiny hummingbirds to large eagles and colorful parrots. They communicate through songs and calls, build nests to protect their eggs and young, and often migrate long distances to find food and suitable climates.
