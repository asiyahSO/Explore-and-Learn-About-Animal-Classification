REPTILES

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals that typically have scaly skin, breathe air through lungs, and lay soft-shelled eggs on land. They include creatures such as snakes, lizards, turtles, and crocodiles. Reptiles are adapted to a variety of habitats including deserts, forests, swamps, and freshwater environments. Most reptiles are ectothermic, meaning they rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature. They have evolved diverse methods for survival, from venomous bites to camouflage and swift movement. Reptiles are among the oldest groups of land vertebrates, with a fossil record dating back millions of years.
