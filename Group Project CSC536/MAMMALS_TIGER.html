BENGAL TIGER

The Bengal tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) is a majestic and powerful big cat native to the Indian subcontinent. Known for its striking orange coat with black stripes, the Bengal tiger is the most numerous tiger subspecies in the wild. They are solitary and territorial animals, primarily hunting large prey such as deer and wild boar. Bengal tigers are excellent swimmers and can thrive in a variety of habitats including tropical forests, mangroves, and grasslands. Unfortunately, due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict, they are listed as endangered by the IUCN Red List. Conservation efforts continue to be critical in preserving the remaining wild populations and their natural habitats.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: BENGAL TIGER
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: PANTHERA TIGRIS
  • TYPE: MAMMALS
  • SIZE: 8 TO 10 FEET (BODY LENGTH, excluding tail)
  • WEIGHT: 220 TO 570 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 8 TO 10 YEARS (In the wild), up to 20 years in captivity
