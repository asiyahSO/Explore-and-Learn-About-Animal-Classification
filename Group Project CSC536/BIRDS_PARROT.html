MACAW PARROT

Macaw Parrots are large, colorful parrots native to the rainforests of South America, especially the Amazon Basin in Brazil and Peru. Famous for their vibrant plumage featuring red, blue, yellow, and green feathers, macaws are highly intelligent and social birds. They use their strong curved beaks to crack nuts and seeds and are known for their loud calls. Macaws play an important role in their ecosystem by dispersing seeds throughout the forest. Many species are threatened due to habitat loss and the pet trade.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: MACAW PARROT
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: Various species within genus Ara
  • TYPE: BIRDS
  • SIZE: 20 TO 40 INCHES (Length)
  • WEIGHT: 1 TO 3 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 30 TO 50 YEARS
