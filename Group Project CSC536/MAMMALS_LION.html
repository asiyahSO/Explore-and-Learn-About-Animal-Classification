ASIATIC LION

The Asiatic lion (Panthera leo persica) is a rare and endangered subspecies of lion found primarily in the Gir Forest of India. Slightly smaller than their African counterparts, Asiatic lions have a distinctive fold of skin along their bellies and a less dense mane. They are social animals, living in prides, and are apex predators, hunting large herbivores such as deer and wild boar. Historically, their range extended from the Middle East to India, but habitat loss and hunting have restricted their population to a small region. Conservation efforts have helped increase their numbers, but the species remains vulnerable due to limited genetic diversity and habitat threats.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: ASIATIC LION
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: PANTHERA LEO PERSICA
  • TYPE: MAMMALS
  • SIZE: 5 TO 8 FEET (Body length including tail)
  • WEIGHT: 330 TO 550 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 12 TO 16 YEARS
