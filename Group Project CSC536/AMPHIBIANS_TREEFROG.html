RED-EYED TREE FROG

The Red-eyed Tree Frog (Agalychnis callidryas) is a strikingly colorful frog native to the tropical rainforests of Central America, including Costa Rica and Nicaragua. Famous for its vivid red eyes, bright green body, and blue and yellow sides, this nocturnal frog uses its appearance to startle predators. Despite their dramatic look, they are not poisonous. Red-eyed tree frogs live in the forest canopy and lay eggs on leaves above water, where the tadpoles drop in after hatching.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: RED-EYED TREE FROG
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: AGALYCHNIS CALLIDRYAS
  • TYPE: AMPHIBIANS
  • SIZE: 2 TO 3 INCHES
  • WEIGHT: 0.2 TO 0.5 OUNCES
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 5 TO 10 YEARS
