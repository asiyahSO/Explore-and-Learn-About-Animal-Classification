COMMON SEAHORSE

The Common Seahorse (Hippocampus kuda) is a small marine fish found worldwide in shallow tropical and temperate waters, often among seagrass beds, coral reefs, and mangroves. Recognizable by their horse-like head and curled tail, seahorses swim upright and use their tails to anchor to vegetation. They feed on small crustaceans and plankton by sucking them into their tubular snouts. Male seahorses are unique in carrying and giving birth to the young.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: COMMON SEAHORSE
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: HIPPOCAMPUS KUDA
  • TYPE: FISH
  • SIZE: UP TO 12 INCHES
  • WEIGHT: LESS THAN 1 POUND
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 1 TO 4 YEARS
