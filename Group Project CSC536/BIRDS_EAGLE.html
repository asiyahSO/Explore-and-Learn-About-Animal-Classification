BALD EAGLE

The Bald Eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus) is a powerful bird of prey native to North America, primarily found in the USA, Canada, and Alaska. Known for its striking white head and tail feathers contrasting with a dark brown body, it is a symbol of strength and freedom. Bald eagles primarily feed on fish, catching them with their strong talons. They build large nests called eyries in tall trees or cliffs near water bodies. Once endangered due to hunting and pesticide use, conservation efforts have helped the population recover, and they are now a conservation success story.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: BALD EAGLE
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: HALIAEETUS LEUCOCEPHALUS
  • TYPE: BIRDS
  • SIZE: 28 TO 40 INCHES (Length)
  • WEIGHT: 6.5 TO 14 POUNDS
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 20 TO 30 YEARS
