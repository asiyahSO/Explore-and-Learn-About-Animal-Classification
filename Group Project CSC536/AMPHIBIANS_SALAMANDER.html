TIGER SALAMANDER

The Tiger Salamander (Ambystoma tigrinum) is one of the largest terrestrial salamanders in North America, found in the USA and Mexico. Named for its striking yellow or green blotches on a dark body, the tiger salamander spends most of its life underground and emerges mostly to breed. They thrive in forests, grasslands, and even agricultural areas, and feed on insects, worms, and small amphibians. Known for their secretive and hardy nature, they are also popular in the pet trade.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: TIGER SALAMANDER
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: AMBYSTOMA TIGRINUM
  • TYPE: AMPHIBIANS
  • SIZE: 6 TO 13 INCHES
  • WEIGHT: 4 TO 8 OUNCES
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 10 TO 15 YEARS
