PANTHER CHAMELEON

The Panther Chameleon (Furcifer pardalis) is a colorful and highly adaptable chameleon species native to Madagascar. Known for their ability to change colors depending on mood, environment, and temperature, panther chameleons display vibrant hues of green, blue, red, and orange. They have independently moving eyes that allow them to look in two directions simultaneously. These insectivores primarily feed on crickets, flies, and other small insects, and rely on their long, sticky tongues to catch prey.

FACTS

  • COMMON NAME: PANTHER CHAMELEON
  • SCIENTIFIC NAME: FURCIFER PARDALIS
  • TYPE: REPTILES
  • SIZE: 6 TO 10 INCHES (Length)
  • WEIGHT: 2 TO 3 OUNCES
  • AVERAGE LIFE SPAN: 5 TO 7 YEARS
